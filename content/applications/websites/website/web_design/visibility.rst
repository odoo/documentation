==========
Visibility
==========

You can choose to display or hide specific content on your website based on conditions of your
choice. These can be entire building blocks or elements within a block.

Go to the **Website editor**: :menuselection:`Website --> Edit`, then follow the instructions below.

Once you have made the desired changes, click on Save to confirm and apply them to your website.

Mobile/desktop
==============

You can hide content depending on whether people are visiting your website from a mobile or desktop
type of device. Click on the block or element you want to hide, and in the :guilabel:`Customize`
tab, scroll down to :guilabel:`Visibility`, and click the :guilabel:`Show/Hide on Mobile` or the
:guilabel:`Show/Hide on Desktop` button.

Conditions
==========

You can also choose other conditions to hide or show building blocks and elements: select one in
the website editor, and in the :guilabel:`Customize` tab, scroll down to :guilabel:`Visibility`,
click on :guilabel:`No condition`, and select :guilabel:`Conditionally` instead. Then, configure the
condition(s) to apply by selecting :guilabel:`Visible for` or :guilabel:`Hidden for`, and click on
:guilabel:`Choose a record` to pick the one(s) you want to show or hide:

- :guilabel:`Country`: the country your visitor is browsing your website from.
- :guilabel:`Languages`: this option is only available if your website is translated into at least
  one language besides the original.
- :guilabel:`UTM Campaign`
- :guilabel:`UTM Medium`
- :guilabel:`UTM Source`
- :guilabel:`Users`: based on whether visitors are logged in, logged out, or everyone.

.. note::
   UTM stands for **Urchin Tracking Module**. It is used primarily for tracking the performance of
   marketing campaigns through links shared in newsletters or social media. For more information on
   link trackers and how to create them in Odoo, please refer to the
   :doc:`Link tracker documentation <../reporting/link_tracker>`.

Invisible elements
==================

The blocks and elements you made hidden are listed at the bottom of the :guilabel:`Blocks`,
:guilabel:`Customize`, and :guilabel:`Edit` menus. Click on them to show them in the website editor.
This allows you to see the elements you have made invisible while working in the website editor, but
it does not change their visibility status in your website's front-end.
